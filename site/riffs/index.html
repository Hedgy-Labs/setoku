Setoku · design exploration

Eighteen divergent riffs on the landing page

Same copy, eighteen aesthetics, each committing hard to a single idea and steering well clear of the usual AI-SaaS look. Built for a Bookface / HN audience that has seen every gradient. Open each in a new tab and compare. Riffs 01 and 10 are refined; 10 has two variations of its own.

Batch 1 · the first five
01 · manual page

setoku(1)

The page as a premium Unix man page. NAME / SYNOPSIS / EXAMPLES, a running header, architecture and revenue chart redrawn in box-drawing characters, demo questions as $ ask examples.

open riff ↗ 02 · preprint

Technical whitepaper

An academic preprint: title block, abstract, numbered sections, Figure 1 / Figure 2, worked examples, footnotes, a serif measure with a drop cap. Confident and un-hyped.

open riff ↗ 03 · drawing sheet

Engineering blueprint

One draftsman’s drawing: graph-paper ground, hairline linework, a title block with drawing number, dimensioned architecture, a bill-of-materials parts list, revision notes.

open riff ↗ 04 · data grid

Spreadsheet-native

The site as a real spreadsheet: formula bar, A–H columns, sheet tabs, a status bar. Demo answers become cells with comment triangles and sticky-note caveats. The product’s native habitat.

open riff ↗ 05 · typographic

Swiss brutalist

International Typographic Style pushed hard: giant grotesk, a strict 12-column grid, hairline rules, margin section numbers, one muted accent. A manifesto poster, not a SaaS page.

Batch 2 · going wider
06 · literate computing

Jupyter notebook

Rendered as setoku.ipynb: a kernel status bar, In[n] / Out[n] cells, the quickstart as code cells, and the demo Q&As as ask(…) cells whose outputs are the real numbers + a matplotlib-style chart.

open riff ↗ 07 · specimen plate

Naturalist field guide

A Victorian botanical monograph: aged paper, the clover as a classified specimen (Kingdom → Etymology), numbered plates, engraved captions, connectors as a checklist of collected species.

open riff ↗ 08 · wayfinding

Transit map

Vignelli signage: the architecture becomes a subway map (ride the MCP line into the gateway interchange), enamel-sign section headers, and an arrivals board for the demo figures.

open riff ↗ 09 · zine

Risograph print

A self-published two-ink riso zine: paper grain, halftone dots, a misregistered headline, rubber-stamped labels, hand-circled numbers. The open-source ethos, made tactile.

open riff ↗ 10 · 1-bit GUI · polished

Classic Macintosh

A black-and-white System 7 desktop, now with real embedded Chicago / Geneva / Monaco bitmap fonts, window scrollbars + grow box, active/inactive title bars, hover-invert menus, a live clock. Two variations below.

Batch 3 · wide + a couple of mono / hacker
11 · mono / hacker

Scene .nfo

A Phrack-style e-zine text file: a figlet SETOKU masthead, a release.nfo block, .-=[ ]=-. dividers, ASCII architecture and chart. Ink on cream, not neon-on-black.

open riff ↗ 12 · mono / hacker

Dev terminal (TUI)

A tasteful dark tiling-terminal: tmux status bar, boxed panes, the demo as a ❯ REPL, block-glyph bar chart. Warm charcoal, one muted-sage accent, blinking caret.

open riff ↗ 13 · wabi-sabi

Japanese editorial

Leans into 設×奥: sumi ink on washi cream, vast negative space, tategaki section markers, a giant ghosted 奥, an inkan seal as the mark, one muted vermilion accent.

open riff ↗ 14 · cartography

Nautical chart

The data lake as an antique Admiralty sea chart: compass rose, rhumb lines, a title cartouche, the architecture as a harbour survey, demo answers as recorded soundings.

open riff ↗ 15 · bookkeeping

Accounting ledger

A bound double-entry daybook: ruled paper, red margin rule, folio headings, the demo Q&As as line-items ruled off to a double-underlined $192M total. Ties to the finance data.

open riff ↗ 16 · curatorial

Museum exhibition

Presented as a curated show (ties to /curate): a title wall, numbered gallery rooms, didactic wall labels with accession numbers, the architecture hung as the principal work.

Classic Macintosh (polished)

The one you liked, refined: real bitmap fonts, scrollbars, grow box, active/inactive windows, a live clock, a Trash icon, window-shade roll-up. The stark 1-bit original.

Platinum

The refined grayscale-3D cousin: pinstripe title bars, beveled buttons and wells, Platinum scrollbars, an application menu. More legible, more “designed,” one muted-sage accent.

Compact Macintosh
