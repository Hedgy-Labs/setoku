# Name search, round 4 — leet droid edition (2026-06-10)

> Brief: "any weird short droidish domains like ru5h or something" — digit-substitution domains where the number reads as a letter (0=o, 1=i/l, 3=e, 4=a, 5=s, 7=t, 8=b, 9=g).

**Method:** 152 leet candidates checked against Verisign whois on 2026-06-10; 37 available. The famous leet spellings are squatted (pr00f, ta11y, ax10m, rat10, tru7h, c0unt, d4ta, m4th, zer0 — and every 4-character combo including t0ku), but one-substitution spellings of *our* vocabulary are wide open. ~$10 each; if one becomes the brand, buy its sibling spellings the same day.

**Practical note:** leet works best as the *agent/product* mark or short link, with a spell-able word domain alongside — there's a verbal tax ("audit, with a one for the i"). As a droid name it's a feature: the substitution IS the robot.

## Pairing idea

The strongest use of these isn't the company name — it's the **droid**: company stays Setoku (or a round-2/3 winner), and the agent you talk to is **AUD-1T**, **REK-0N**, or **L3MMA**, living at the matching domain. That gets the R2-D2 energy without putting a spelling tax on the corporate front door.
